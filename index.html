Do You Hear It?

The whispers started at midnight. They were faint at first, like the rustling of leaves in an empty forest. But soon, they became voices—muttering, gasping, pleading. I tried to ignore them, thinking it was my imagination. But then the scratching began.

Something was inside the walls.

I pressed my ear against the cold plaster, my breath hitching. The scratching grew louder, more frantic. It wasn't a rat. It wasn't the pipes. It was something else. And then, I heard my name.

"Come closer," it whispered.

My blood ran cold. I stumbled back, but my body refused to run. The lights flickered, and in that brief moment of darkness, I felt something behind me. Something breathing.

The TV screen turned on by itself. Static filled the room. And in the flickering light, I saw movement—shadowy, crawling figures on the walls. Their faces were wrong. Twisted. Hungry. The whispers turned into laughter.

"Play the video," they urged.

My hands shook as I reached for my phone. The file was already open. The play button was waiting.

And then, the final whisper came. Closer than before. Right against my ear.

"Listen..."

The screen went black. The whispers stopped. And then, in the silence, something else began to breathe.
